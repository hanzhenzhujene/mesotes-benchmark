# Philosophical Framework

MESOTES operationalizes a subset of Aristotelian ethics for benchmark design. The goal is practical evaluation structure, not a claim that the benchmark captures the whole of Aristotelian moral psychology.

## Sphere

A `sphere` is the relevant domain of action or feeling being regulated in the case: fear, appetite, anger, truthfulness, resource use, honor, humor, or ordinary social conduct. The benchmark asks models to locate the case in the right sphere before they classify candidate responses.

## Deficiency, excess, and mean

The benchmark treats the `mean` as the proportionate response relative to the agent and the particulars of the case. It is not a neutral midpoint between boldness and timidity, candor and tact, or austerity and indulgence. The right response depends on who is acting, toward whom, when, for what end, and under what constraints.

`deficiency` and `excess` are therefore not mere "too little" and "too much" in a numeric sense. Either can arise through public exposure, delay, evasiveness, overconfidence, harshness, indulgence, or role-neglect, depending on the scenario.

## False midpoint

A `false midpoint` is a candidate action that looks balanced because it softens both extremes, splits the difference, or avoids strong action. In Aristotelian terms, it is still the wrong response because it misses the salient particulars. This is one of the central stress tests in MESOTES.

## Phronesis salience

`phronesis_salience` is an operational label for how much the item depends on discerning and weighing the right particulars. It is a benchmark convenience, not a claim that practical wisdom can be cleanly separated from virtue in Aristotle's own theory.

- `low`: a well-specified case where the relevant considerations are comparatively clear
- `medium`: correct judgment depends on multiple particulars but the case is still mostly determinate
- `high`: the case depends strongly on salient particulars, role-relative judgment, or recognizing that more information is required

## Needs-more-info cases

Some scenarios are deliberately under-specified. In those items, the best response is to ask, check, or delay until the relevant particulars are known. This prevents the benchmark from rewarding confident verdicts when good judgment should instead acknowledge an information gap.

## No-mean exceptions

MESOTES includes cases where the benchmarked scenario turns on an act-kind that should not be treated as becoming good by being done moderately. The benchmark still labels the best response to the situation, but it does not treat the underlying act itself as admitting a virtuous midpoint.

## Contemporary operationalization

This repository separates two claims:

- canonical Aristotle is a matter for textual and philosophical scholarship
- MESOTES is a contemporary benchmark operationalization designed for model evaluation

That means the released docs may sometimes use modern domain labels, structured tags, and annotation shortcuts that make the benchmark easier to use without claiming strict historical purity.
